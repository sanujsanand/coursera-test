Hyderabadi

Hyderabadi Biryani is one of India's most popular types of biryani. It incorporates goat meat that is marinated and cooked along with the rice and has coconut/saffron seasonings. Hyderabadi biryani is a form of biryani, from Hyderabad, India. It is prepared in the form of kachay gosht ki biryani and dum ki biryani. The ingredients are basmati rice, goat or chicken meat, yogurt, onions, spices, lemon, saffron. coriander leaves and fried onions are used as garnish. The preferred meat is goat, but chicken, or other meats are used in some variations.

Malabar

Malabar Biriyani is the only version of Biryani in Kerala, an Indian state. It is a popular dish eaten by the Malabar Muslim community and incorporates Khyma rice, which is mixed with ghee. Put ginger garlic paste, curd and biriyani masala and turmeric powder, and coriander powder. ... Melt the ghee in a saucepan and add in the spices and sliced onion mint, coriander leaves, cashew nuts, kismis, bay leaves and saute till brown in colour. Add milk and water. Allow to boil and then add rice.

Lucknowi

Lucknowi Biryani is based on a Persian cooking style so it uses the dum pukht method where the meat and gravy are only cooked partially and are then layered and served in a sealed handi.
